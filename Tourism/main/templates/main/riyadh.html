Riyadh city

Welcome to Riyadh, the birthplace of modern Saudi Arabia, where old-world charm meets 21st-century vision. Riyadh means "gardens," a nod to the region's past that dates back to the 14th century, when the lands were fertile with plenty of water channels and trees. Nowadays, Riyadh is known as the central region, and embraces the nation's capital: a modern metropolis with a financial and commercial center, and a growing cultural scene. It is a city rich in its history, with countless forts, palaces, and museums, as well as the most diverse bazaars in the country. It is also home to the oldest university in Saudi Arabia, King Saud University, as well as one of the tallest skyscrapers in the world, the Kingdom Tower, and more than 4,000 mosques.

History of the Riyadh Region: Ad-Diriyah and At-Turaif

The larger region also offers a wealth of tourist attractions, including Diriyah, the original capital of the first Saudi state. It is an area worth visiting located in the northwest of the capital, and home to a carefully restored mud-stone neighborhood known as Al-Turaif. It was nominated for registration on the UNESCO World Heritage List in 2010, and this neighborhood offers a unique glimpse into the country's heritage. Wander the Museum of Social Life, with galleries dotted among the ruins, for a dose of history and learning. While you're in the area, take time to visit the restaurants and cafes open along the winding alleys of the nearby Bujairi neighborhood.

Tuwaiq Mountain

Those looking for peace and quiet should head to the edge of the world in the north of the city, where the sprawling cliffs of Jabal Tuwaiq offer stunning views of the arid plains below.
