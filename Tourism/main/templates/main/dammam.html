The Eastern Region Of Saudi Arabia: The City Of Dammam

The city of Dammam represents the characteristics of scarcity, originality and wealth, which made it the pearl of the Arabian Gulf, as its strategic location in the eastern region carried great importance in the economy of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, as its lands included the first oil well that produced promising commercial quantities that allowed Saudi Arabia to lead the scene of global oil markets until the present day. The well was called “The Well of Goodness” and is also known as Dammam Well No. 7. The city combines modern and renewed destinations, fragrant and originality in popular markets adorned with exquisite traditional crafts, such as: weaving weaving, pottery and wickerwork, as well as discovering in its beaches the pleasure of refreshing summer activities, Exciting cruises. The residents of Dammam are characterized by a friendly nature and cultural openness coming from the location of their city on the Gulf. Since 1986, the King Fahd Causeway, the link between Dammam and the Kingdom of Bahrain, has extended its bonds to form a gateway for communication and cultural integration.
