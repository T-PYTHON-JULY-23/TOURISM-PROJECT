Let's Start Your Journey to Saudi Arabia

Explore the wonders of Saudi Arabia, where modern cities blend with ancient history. From Riyadh's marvels to Red Sea landscapes, it's a land of contrasts. Immerse in warm hospitality, savor Arabian cuisine, and witness iconic landmarks. Uncover hidden treasures, embark on cultural discoveries, and create unforgettable memories in this remarkable nation.

Al-Soudah Mountains in Abha

Perched amidst the enchanting Al-Soudah Mountains, Abha unveils a breathtaking landscape that mesmerizes with its rugged allure. The region boasts a temperate climate, owing to its elevated position, offering a cool retreat from the heat of the area.

QASSIM

Qassim Province, also known as the Qassim Region, is a central Saudi Arabian province with an area of 58,046 km² and a population of 1,370,727. Revered as the "alimental basket" of the country due to its agricultural prowess, it boasts over 400 settlements and a thriving capital, Buraydah, housing 60% of its population. Notably, Qassim holds the lowest poverty rate in Saudi Arabia and is rich in cultural heritage and urban development.

TROJENA

Trojena is part of NEOM’s regional plan, located 50km from the Gulf of Aqaba coast in the heart of our nature region. Elevations range from 1,500m to 2,600m and cover an area of nearly 60 square kilometers.

FARASAN ISLANDS

Farasan Island offers captivating sites, including the Roman-era village of Al Qassar and Beit Al Refai with its Farasani architecture. Explore the unique Najdi Mosque and enjoy fishing for a memorable experience. Access the island via the Jazan ferry, with opportunities for snorkeling and camping. Witness stunning gazelles against the mountains during a beachside camp, all contributing to an unforgettable Farasan adventure.

UMLUJ CITY

Umluj City, nestled along the Red Sea, enchants with pristine beaches and vibrant marine life. Known as the "Pearl of the Red Sea," it offers a haven for water enthusiasts and those seeking tranquility. With its untouched beauty and azure waters, Umluj beckons as a serene coastal escape.

Madinah Al-Munawarah

Madinah Al-Munawarah, known as the "City of the Prophet," holds deep spiritual significance as the burial place of Prophet Muhammad. Pilgrims and visitors are drawn to its serene atmosphere, with the Prophet's Mosque at its heart. The city's peaceful ambiance and rich history make it a cherished destination for Muslims and a source of tranquility and reflection.
